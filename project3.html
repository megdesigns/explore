Introduction

Popcorn Park Zoo

The Popcorn Park Animal Refuge is a unique zoo experience where visitors are able to see and read the special stories of each animal and how they were rescued from "abandonment, cruelty, injury, illness, handicap, exploitation, old age, inappropriate ownership or who could not be safely returned to the wild".

All current information for this site is housed under a tab on the Associated Humane Societies current website (ahscares.org).

This provided us with an opportunity to create an impactful stand alone site to attract visitors and provide clean and clear information about this amazing Zoo!

Description

Purpose

A redesigned, mobile-optimized website for Popcorn Park Zoo - an independent site for the zoo which currently only has information housed on Associated Humane Society's site.

Functionality

A standalone website experience with attention to mobile usability. Refreshed, focused platform design which allows visitors to explore animal profiles with rich imagery and backstories, buy admission tickets, easily plan visits, learn about the zoo's mission and the animals they care for, and make donations or adopt/sponsor animals to show support.

Our Design

Modern Visual Design

We updated the typography, color scheme, and layout to replace the outdated aesthetic of the current site.

Emotional Storytelling

We dedicated animal pages to connect users with real rescue stories.

Support and Involvement

We showed clearly structured donation and adoption flows to encourage patronage and enhance user engagement.

Design Feedback

We gathered feedback on our initial prototype designs from two potential users and adjusted our designs to address issues that were noted.

User 1: "Buy Tickets" & "Events" Cards

"Buy Tickets" and "Events" headers appeared as clickable buttons though they were not clickable.
Our revised design displays section labels more clearly to differentiate them from actual call-to-action buttons to reduce misinterpretation and improve clarity.

User 2: Donate button in navigation

Our initial navigation emphasized the "Donate" option by placing it in a standalone button in our hamburger menu. While the intention was to highlight its importance, the user felt like it was not consistent with the rest of the navigation and felt disconnected.
We repositioned "Donate" as a standard list item within the menu to feel more cohesive and present a unified set of actions rather than split elements within our navigation.

Design Justification

Advancing functionality and aesthetics through a mobile friendly site to draw visitors to the Popcorn Park Zoo. Empowering users to learn more, get involved, and contribute with fewer barriers.

Existing Website:

  • Visually outdated
  • Difficult to navigate
  • Limited mobile functionality
  • Users need to dig through layers of generic AHS content to find Popcorn Park-specific information

Design Decisions:

  • Modernized interface with a clean, cohesive layout, rounded UI elements, subtle drop shadows, and high-contrast buttons to improve readability and interaction
  • Visual hierarchy to make content easy to scan and navigate through clear labeling, bold section headings, and generous spacing

Design Approach:

  • Mobile app did not fit the needs of the organization
  • Pivoted to a mobile-first website approach
  • Mobile site design offers accessibility without demanding download and updates as an app may require
  • SEO friendly website will be more discoverable increasing potential traffic, visibility and donations

Prioritizing mobile access:

  • Structured each page to function seamlessly on small screens, emphasizing vertically stacked content, touch-friendly components, and quick load times
  • Simplified hamburger menu organizes content logically while maintaining a clutter-free appearance
